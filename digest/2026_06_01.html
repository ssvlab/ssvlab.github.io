A comprehensive survey tracing ESBMC’s trajectory from its 2009 origins to the state of the art in 2026, covering the expansion to nine language front-ends (C, C++, CUDA, Kotlin, Python, Rust, Solidity, and more), 43 competition awards at SV-COMP and Test-Comp, and deep integration with industrial SMT solvers. ESBMC v8.3 – Official Stable Release

ESBMC Development Team • 14 May 2026 • GitHub

ESBMC v8.3 delivers a substantial expansion of the Python frontend with broad tuple and set support, comprehensive dataclass conformance, and with/async with lowering via preprocessor desugaring, alongside new operational models for collections.deque, OrderedDict, random, and time modules. The C/C++ front-end gains C++17 structured bindings, C++20 class template argument deduction, C++23 explicit object parameters, the C++20 spaceship operator, and full Clang 22 compatibility. Verification improvements include CWE mapping with SARIF output, violation-witness validation, a new k-path coverage mode, and promotion of Bitwuzla to the default SMT solver; the simplifier was also rewritten with reassociation support.

Software Verification & Formal Methods

Verification of Configurable SRA Systems

Alessandro Cimatti, Alberto Griggio, Christian Lidström, Gianluca Redondi, Dylan Trenti • ~20–26 May 2026 • arXiv

Addresses the formal verification of systems composed of collections of asynchronous processes orchestrated by domain-specific schedulers (scheduler-restricted asynchronous systems, SRA), where process–process and process–scheduler interactions make verification difficult. Introduces a contract-based, deductive verification approach that combines compositional proof rules abstracting the scheduler with automatic summarisation of scheduler-invoked methods, supporting parameterised verification of configurable SRA families. The method proves at once that every legal instantiation of a configurable SRA satisfies its top-level invariant properties, a significant step toward scalable concurrent-system verification.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21385

Knowledge Graphs, the Missing Link in Agentic AI-based Formal Verification

Vaisakh Naduvodi Viswambharan et al. • 7 May 2026 • arXiv

Proposes a verification-centric knowledge-graph (KG) framework to improve the quality of SystemVerilog Assertions (SVAs) generated by LLM-based agentic workflows for hardware formal verification. The KG is constructed from structured intermediate representations capturing specification intent, RTL hierarchy, generated property candidates, and formal-tool feedback (syntax diagnostics, counterexamples, coverage reports), grounding the LLM in precise semantic context. Results demonstrate consistently syntactically valid and runnable SVAs across all evaluated designs with minimal correction effort, effectively mitigating undeclared identifiers, macro mismatches, and incorrect hierarchical references.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06434

Towards Formal Verification of Hybrid Synchronous Programs with Refinement Types

Serra Z. Dane, Jiawei Chen, Marc Pouzet, Jean-Baptiste Jeannin • 6 May 2026 • arXiv

Extends formal verification of synchronous programs to hybrid systems—combining discrete-time control logic with differential equations—using refinement types, targeting safety-critical cyber-physical systems such as autonomous vehicles and aircraft where executable controller code must be both formally verified and deployable. Builds on an earlier refinement-type framework for strictly discrete-time synchronous languages and augments it to handle continuous dynamics, enabling precise modelling of a wider variety of real-world environments. The approach promises a path from abstract safety proofs to directly executable hybrid controller implementations without a separate code-generation gap.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04377
