ESBMC: A Survey of Its Evolution, Integration, and Future Directions in Formal Software Verification

Pierre Dantas • Lucas Cordeiro • Waldir Junior  •  arXiv  •  25 May 2026 (v1); revised 30 May 2026 (v2)

This survey traces ESBMC’s trajectory from its first publication in 2009 as a research prototype for embedded ANSI-C verification into one of the most versatile formal verification platforms available, now supporting nine language front-ends (C, C++, CUDA, CHERI, Kotlin, Python, Rust, Solidity, and WebAssembly). The paper documents the tool’s integration with industrial-strength SMT solvers and, most recently, its coupling with large language models and autonomous AI agents for tasks such as self-healing software and loop invariant generation, including the first industrial deployment through the NVIDIA-OpenSMA framework. It records 43 competition awards at SV-COMP and Test-COMP, including 2nd place in SV-COMP 2026 ReachSafety and FuSeBMC winning 1st Overall at Test-COMP 2026 for the fourth consecutive year.

Bounded Model Checking (BMC)

Finding Software Vulnerabilities in Large C Projects via Bounded Model Checking

J. O. De Sousa • B. C. de Farias • T. A. Da Silva et al.  •  International Journal on Software Tools for Technology Transfer (STTT), Springer  •  2026 (online-first; exact month unconfirmed)

This paper confronts the scalability bottleneck that limits BMC tools to small and medium codebases, presenting a practical methodology for applying BMC at scale to real-world C software. The methodology combines automated source-code preprocessing, a guided verification workflow, and a function-level prioritisation mechanism that focuses verification effort on code sections most likely to harbour defects, reducing state-space explosion without sacrificing coverage. An evaluation on popular open-source C projects discovered security vulnerabilities subsequently confirmed by the respective upstream maintainers, validating the approach’s practical utility.

https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10009-026-00838-0

Software Verification & Formal Methods

ConVer: Using Contracts and Loop Invariant Synthesis for Scalable Formal Software Verification

Muhammad A. A. Pirzada • Weiqi Wang • Yiannis Charalambous • Konstantin Korovin • Lucas C. Cordeiro  •  arXiv  •  26 May 2026

ConVer is a top-down compositional verification tool designed to overcome state-space explosion in BMC by using a large language model to synthesise function contracts from a given system-level property, then alternating between system-level and function-level SMT-based checks in a CEGAR–CEGIS refinement loop that refines contracts whenever a check fails via SMART ICE learning. The approach combines the natural-language reasoning power of LLMs for contract generation with the formal correctness guarantees of SMT solving, enabling verification of larger programs without manual annotation effort. On the Frama-C benchmark of 45 simple C programs, ConVer achieves 82–96 % verification success across three LLM backends, with 93–95 % of converged programs requiring only a single CEGAR–CEGIS iteration.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.27051
