Pierre Dantas, Lucas Cordeiro, Waldir Junior • arXiv preprint (arXiv:2605.26169) • 25 May 2026 (rev. 30 May 2026)

This comprehensive survey traces ESBMC’s journey from a 2009 research prototype for embedded ANSI-C verification into a multi-language platform that today supports nine programming-language front-ends and integrates industrial-strength SMT solvers. The paper documents 43 competitive awards at SV-COMP and Test-Comp (including a 1st-place Test-Comp 2026 result via FuSeBMC), Distinguished Paper Awards at ICSE’11 and ASE’24, and the tool’s first industrial deployment at Lockheed Martin through an agentic model-checking architecture. It also charts recent work coupling ESBMC with Large Language Models for self-healing software and automated program repair, and the founding of the VeriBee spin-off backed by over £9.3 million in confirmed public research funding.

ESBMC-PLC: Formal Verification of IEC 61131-3 Ladder Diagram Programs Using SMT-Based Model Checking

Pierre Dantas, Lucas Cordeiro, Waldir Junior • arXiv preprint (arXiv:2606.15461) • June 2026

ESBMC-PLC is introduced as the first open-source formal verifier with native support for IEC 61131-3 Ladder Diagram programs in the standard PLCopen XML format, implemented as a new frontend for ESBMC. The tool translates LD rungs to ESBMC’s GOTO intermediate representation, models the PLC scan cycle as a while(true) loop with nondeterministic inputs, and checks safety properties via SMT-based bounded model checking or k-induction using a concise five-property YAML specification language (mutual exclusion, invariant, absence, response, reachability). Evaluation on 13 benchmarks drawn from six industrial domains — including deployed CONTROLLINO PLCs and MathWorks Simulink PLC Coder — demonstrates correct classification across all 61 properties, closing two of the four research gaps identified in a systematic survey of 22 studies from 2020–2026.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15461

Federated Formal Verification: Cross-Backend Citation, Cross-Axis Convergence, and AI-Orchestrated Proof Dispatch for Production Systems

Pierre Falda • arXiv preprint (arXiv:2606.02019) • 1 June 2026

This paper proposes a “federated” architecture for formal verification in which proof obligations are dispatched across multiple heterogeneous verification backends coordinated by an AI orchestration layer. The work introduces cross-backend citation mechanisms, allowing proofs produced by one verifier to be cited as lemmas by another, and explores axis-convergence strategies to improve scalability for production-scale systems. The approach represents an emerging line of research at the intersection of AI-assisted proof management and classical formal methods infrastructure.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02019
