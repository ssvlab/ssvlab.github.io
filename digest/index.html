ESBMC: A Survey of Its Evolution, Integration, and Future Directions in Formal Software Verification

Pierre Dantas, Lucas Cordeiro (University of Manchester), Waldir Junior (Federal University of Amazonas) • arXiv (2605.26169) • 30 May 2026

A comprehensive retrospective and forward-looking survey tracing ESBMC from its 2009 origins as an embedded-C verifier into a platform now supporting nine language front-ends: C, C++, CUDA, CHERI, Kotlin, Python, Rust, Solidity, and IEC 61131-3 PLC programs. The paper documents 43 competition awards at SV-COMP and Test-COMP — including 1st Overall in Test-COMP 2026 and 2nd in SV-COMP 2026 ReachSafety — as well as an industrial deployment at Lockheed Martin and the recent integration of LLMs and autonomous AI agents into the ESBMC-AI workflow. Future directions outlined include deeper LLM coupling for automated vulnerability repair, expanded multi-language support, and broader industrial outreach.

ESBMC-PLC: Formal Verification of IEC 61131-3 Ladder Diagram Programs Using SMT-Based Model Checking

Pierre Dantas, Lucas Cordeiro (University of Manchester), Waldir Junior (Federal University of Amazonas) • arXiv (2606.15461) • 13 Jun 2026

Introduces ESBMC-PLC, the first open-source formal verifier with native Ladder Diagram (LD) support in the standard PLCopen XML format, implemented as a new ESBMC front-end. The tool translates LD rungs into GOTO intermediate representation, models the PLC scan cycle as a non-terminating loop with nondeterministic inputs, and verifies safety properties via SMT-based bounded model checking or k-induction. A five-property YAML specification language covering mutual exclusion, invariant, absence, response, and reachability is provided so industrial practitioners can express requirements without needing to learn temporal logic formalisms.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15461

ESBMC-GraphPLC: Formal Verification of Graphical PLCopen XML Ladder Diagram Programs Using SMT-Based Model Checking

Pierre Dantas, Lucas Cordeiro, Waldir Junior • arXiv (2606.18941) • 17 Jun 2026 (latest revision: 22 Jun 2026)

A direct follow-up to ESBMC-PLC that closes a critical soundness gap: ESBMC-PLC produced empty GOTO IR when processing graphical PLCopen XML exports from CONTROLLINO, Beremiz, or OpenPLC Editor, resulting in vacuously successful and therefore unsound verification outcomes. ESBMC-GraphPLC addresses this by introducing a DFS-based graphical Ladder Diagram resolver that correctly parses rung topologies in graphical XML before conversion to GOTO IR. Together the two tools provide end-to-end formal verification coverage for the dominant PLC programming notation across both textual and graphical authoring environments.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18941

IsabeLLM: Automated Theorem Proving Applied to Formally Verifying Consensus

Elliot Jones, William Knottenbelt • arXiv (2606.18098) • 16 Jun 2026

Applies LLM-driven automated theorem proving within the Isabelle proof assistant to formal verification of distributed consensus protocols, which must guarantee that nodes reach agreement in potentially adversarial environments. The paper extends the IsabeLLM tool with Retrieval-Augmented Generation, structured error tracing, and counterexample generation to reduce hallucinations and guide proof agents through complex verification goals more reliably. This work occupies the same LLM-meets-formal-verification space that ESBMC-AI explores and provides complementary benchmarks for evaluating agent-guided proof search strategies in a theorem-proving rather than model-checking setting.

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18098
